In this article, we explore the top 10 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) that every HR Manager must monitor to understand organizational health and workforce efficiency.

---

### 1. Turnover Rate
Measures the percentage of employees leaving the organization. A sudden spike in this number is an "early warning sign" of issues in the work environment or uncompetitive compensation.
* **Formula:** (Number of employees who left ÷ Average number of employees) × 100

### 2. Employee Productivity Rate
The scale that balances employee effort against actual output. This metric helps identify training needs or the need for task redistribution.
* **Formula:** Total Output ÷ Total number of employees

### 3. Salary Increase Rate
A vital indicator for annual budget planning. It helps balance growth costs while maintaining the organization's financial efficiency.
* **Formula:** (Total Salary Increases ÷ Total Payroll) × 100

### 4. Revenue Per Employee
One of the most powerful metrics to showcase HR's value to executive management, measuring the direct financial return for every individual in the system.
* **Formula:** Total Revenue ÷ Total number of employees

### 5. Employee Engagement Score
The number that reflects the "pulse of the organization." Engaged employees are not only more productive but are also less likely to leave and more loyal to the brand.
* **Formula:** Derived from the average scores of engagement and job satisfaction surveys.

### 6. Absenteeism Rate
When this rate exceeds normal limits, it often indicates low morale or unhealthy work pressure that affects business continuity.
* **Formula:** (Number of days absent ÷ Total number of scheduled working days) × 100

### 7. Training Cost Per Employee
Investing in people is the safest investment. This metric determines how serious the organization is about developing its cadres to keep pace with market challenges.
* **Formula:** Total Training Cost ÷ Number of employees trained

### 8. Time To Fill
Time is money. Every day a position remains vacant is a day of lost productivity. This figure measures the efficiency of your talent acquisition team.
* **Formula:** Total days taken to fill positions ÷ Number of positions filled

### 9. Offer Acceptance Rate
Are your job offers attractive enough? This metric tells you the strength of your Employer Branding in the labor market.
* **Formula:** (Number of offers accepted ÷ Total number of offers extended) × 100

### 10. Cost Per Hire
Cost-saving starts here. This includes all expenses from advertising to interview hours and the initial onboarding of the new employee.
* **Formula:** (Total recruitment cost + Total onboarding cost) ÷ Number of new hires

---

### Final Thought
Numbers don't lie, but they require an expert to read between the lines. Transforming these metrics into tangible reality is what distinguishes a traditional manager from a strategic leader who guides their organization toward sustainable growth.
