# Weekly Rest and Leave Entitlements Under Saudi Labor Law: A Complete Guide
 
A question that comes up constantly in HR offices: "My brother's wedding is tomorrow — can I take my weekly rest day instead of using my annual leave?" Or from the company's side: "An employee resigned after using half of their annual leave. Do we deduct the rest from their final pay?"
 
Questions like these have precise answers under Saudi Labor Law, but a lot of the confusion comes from mixing up the different types of leave and the conditions attached to each. This guide covers rest periods, the weekly rest day, and all eight types of leave provided for by law, written to serve both HR professionals and employees.
 
---
 
## Part One: Rest Periods During the Working Day
 
Article 106 sets out two core rules:
 
- An employee may not work more than **five consecutive hours** without a rest break (for prayer and meals) of at least **half an hour** each time
- An employee may not remain at the workplace for more than **12 hours** in a single day
Article 107 clarifies an important point: rest, prayer, and meal breaks are **not counted as actual working hours**, the employee is not under the employer's authority during them, and the employer may not require the employee to remain at the workplace during these breaks.
 
**Exception:** Article 108 gives the Minister authority to designate specific jobs where continuous work without a break is technically necessary due to operational conditions. Even so, the employer remains obligated to provide time for prayer and meals in a manner organized by the establishment during the work itself.

## Part Two: The Weekly Rest Day
 
### The Basic Rule
 
Article 104: **Friday is the weekly rest day for all employees**, subject to two conditions:
 
- Full pay
- Not less than **24 consecutive hours**
### Can Friday Be Substituted?
 
Yes, but under specific conditions:
 
- The employer must **notify the relevant labor office** first
- The substitution applies to **some employees** (not necessarily all) with any other day of the week
- Those employees must be enabled to **fulfill their religious duties**
- **Cash compensation for the weekly rest day is never permitted, under any circumstances** — this is a point many employers get wrong, mistakenly treating it like overtime pay
### Exception for Remote Locations
 
Article 105 allows, in locations far from urban centers and in work requiring continuous operations, **accumulating weekly rest periods** for a period not exceeding **8 weeks**, on condition that:
 
- The employer and employees agree to it
- The Ministry approves it
- The accumulated rest period is calculated starting from the employees' arrival at the nearest city with transport access, ending at the time of their return
This exception typically applies to remote-site work such as mining or construction projects far from populated areas.

## Part Three: The Eight Types of Leave
 
The table below summarizes the main types, followed by a detailed breakdown of each:
 
| Leave Type | Duration | Pay | Core Condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| Annual | 21 days (30 days after 5 continuous years of service) | Full, paid in advance | Cannot be deferred without approval, or cashed out |
| Public holidays | Eid Al-Fitr 4 days, Eid Al-Adha 4 days, National Day 1 day, Founding Day 1 day | Full | Set annually via regulation, based on the Umm Al-Qura calendar |
| Social occasions | 5 days (marriage / death of spouse or ascendant/descendant), 3 days (death of sibling or birth of a child) | Full | Employer may request supporting documents |
| Hajj | 10 to 15 days (inclusive of Eid Al-Adha) | Full | Once during employment, after 2 continuous years of service |
| Sick leave | 30 days + 60 days + 30 days (120 days per year) | Full, then 3/4, then unpaid | Illness must be proven; the "year" starts from the first sick day |
| Exam leave | Actual exam days | Full (non-repeated year) or unpaid (repeated year) | Request submitted at least 15 days in advance + prior approval of enrollment |
| Unpaid leave | By agreement | Unpaid | Requires employer's approval; contract is suspended if it exceeds 20 days |
 
### 1) Annual Leave (Articles 109–111)
 
- **21 days** per year, rising to **30 days** once the employee has completed 5 continuous years with the same employer
- Paid **in advance**, before the leave begins
- **Must** be taken within the year it's earned; it cannot be waived or cashed out during employment
- The employer sets the dates according to operational needs or on a rotating basis, but must notify the employee of the confirmed date at least **30 days** in advance
**Deferral:**
- The employee may defer leave to the following year, with the employer's approval
- The employer may defer it after the end of the entitlement year if operational needs require, for up to **90 days**; if a longer deferral is needed, it requires the employee's **written consent**, and in no case may the deferral extend past the end of the year following the entitlement year
**On leaving the job:** the employee is entitled to payment for unused leave days, as well as leave pay for partial years worked, proportional to time actually served.
 
> **Example:** An employee works 4 months of the year, then resigns. They are entitled to leave pay equal to 4/12 of their annual entitlement (21 or 30 days, depending on total tenure), regardless of whether they used any of it.
 
### 2) Public Holiday and National Occasion Leave (Article 112 and its regulation)
 
- **Eid Al-Fitr:** 4 days, starting the day after the 29th of Ramadan (Umm Al-Qura calendar)
- **Eid Al-Adha:** 4 days, starting from the Day of Arafah
- **National Day:** 1 day
- **Founding Day:** 1 day (February 22, Gregorian calendar)
**Overlap rules — a frequently asked point:**
 
| If a holiday overlaps with | What happens? |
|---|---|
| The weekly rest day | The employee is compensated with an equivalent day before or after the holiday |
| Annual leave | Annual leave is extended by the number of holiday days |
| Sick leave | The employee receives **full pay** for the holiday days, regardless of the sick-leave pay tier otherwise applicable |
| National Day or Founding Day with one of the Eid holidays | The employee is **not compensated** for that day |
 
### 3) Social Occasion Leave (Article 113)
 
- **5 days**, full pay: marriage, or death of a spouse, or death of an ascendant or descendant
- **3 days**, full pay: death of a sibling
- **3 days**, full pay: birth of a child, to be used within 7 days of the birth
- All are calculated from the **date of the event** itself, and the employer may request supporting documentation (marriage contract, death certificate, birth certificate)
### 4) Hajj Leave (Article 114)
 
- **10 to 15 days**, inclusive of the Eid Al-Adha holiday
- Full pay
- **Once only, during the entire period of employment** — not granted if the employee has already performed Hajj
- Eligibility condition: the employee must have completed **at least 2 continuous years** with the employer
- The employer may set the number of employees granted this leave each year according to operational needs (to avoid work disruption if everyone applies at the same time)
### 5) Exam Leave (Article 115)
 
This is one of the more nuanced types, since its rules branch depending on the situation:
 
- **If the employer approved the employee's enrollment** in an educational institution: the employee is entitled to **full-paid** leave for a non-repeated academic year, for the actual number of exam days. If the year is a repeat, the leave is **unpaid**.
- **If the employer did not approve the enrollment**: the employee may use annual leave if available, or take unpaid leave for the actual exam days.
Additional conditions: the request must be submitted at least 15 days in advance, and the employee forfeits pay for the leave if it's proven they did not actually sit the exam (without prejudice to the employer's right to take disciplinary action separately).
 
### 6) Unpaid Leave (Article 116)
 
- Requires the employer's approval, with duration set by mutual agreement
- If it exceeds **20 days**, the employment contract is considered **suspended** for the duration of the leave (meaning its effects are paused), unless the parties agree otherwise
### 7) Sick Leave (Article 117)
 
A tiered system every HR professional should have memorized:
 
| Period | Pay |
|---|---|
| First 30 days | Full pay |
| Next 60 days | 3/4 pay |
| Final 30 days | Unpaid |
 
**Total: up to 120 days per year**, whether taken consecutively or in separate periods. The "year" here is calculated from the **date of the first sick leave day** — not the calendar or Hijri year.
 
### 8) A General Restriction on All Leave Types (Article 118)
 
An employee may not work for another employer while on any of the leaves covered above. If the original employer proves this occurred, they may withhold the employee's pay for the leave period, or recover any pay already disbursed.

## Practical Guidance for HR Professionals
 
1. **Document the labor office's approval** before substituting Friday as the weekly rest day for any group of employees
2. **Never pay cash in lieu of the weekly rest day** — even if the employee requests it willingly — since it's a matter of public order that cannot be waived
3. **Send annual leave notices in writing at least 30 days in advance**, not verbally, to avoid future disputes over timing
4. **Cross-reference the annual leave schedule with the public holiday calendar** to avoid miscalculations when they overlap
5. **Don't grant Hajj leave independent of the two-year condition** — many companies mistakenly grant it from year one, assuming it's an automatic right
6. **Track each employee's individual sick-leave "year" separately** — it starts from their own first sick day, not a company-wide date
---
 
## Common Mistakes That Lead to Labor Disputes
 
1. Paying cash in lieu of an actual weekly rest day
2. Treating official holidays as part of the annual leave balance instead of extending it
3. Granting Hajj leave before the employee completes two continuous years of service
4. Denying sick-leave pay entirely instead of applying the tiered structure (30/60/30 days)
5. Refusing to pay for unused annual leave upon termination of employment
6. Failing to notify the employee of their annual leave date within the required 30-day window
7. Allowing an employee to work for another employer during leave without oversight, then facing claims later

## Quick Checklist
 
- [ ] Is the weekly rest day full-paid and no less than 24 consecutive hours?
- [ ] Was the labor office notified before substituting Friday for any employee?
- [ ] Is each employee's annual leave balance up to date (21 or 30 days based on tenure)?
- [ ] Was the annual leave notice sent in writing at least 30 days in advance?
- [ ] Is the public holiday calendar correctly cross-referenced against overlap rules?
- [ ] Has the employee completed two years of service before Hajj leave is granted?
- [ ] Is the tiered sick-leave pay structure (full / three-quarters / unpaid) being applied correctly?
- [ ] Are all social occasion leave requests backed by supporting documentation?
Understanding the fine distinctions between leave types isn't just administrative diligence — it's direct protection for the company against labor disputes, and at the same time a genuine safeguard of the rights the law guarantees every employee
