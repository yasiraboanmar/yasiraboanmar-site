Before February 2025, Saudi Labor Law contained no legal definition of the word "resignation" — a gap that fueled countless disputes and allowed some employers to weaponize undated resignation letters against employees in cases of conflict.

The February 2025 amendment introduced **Article 79 bis** to close this gap and establish clear rules protecting both parties. But the most important thing — and what most people miss — is that this article draws a fundamental distinction between two types of contracts.

---

## Part One: The Core Distinction Between Contract Types

This is the key to understanding everything that follows.

### Fixed-Term Contract → Resignation (Article 79 bis)

The new legal definition of resignation in Saudi Labor Law states explicitly: **"a written expression by the employee, free from coercion, of their desire to terminate a fixed-term employment contract."**

In precise legal terms, resignation applies **exclusively to fixed-term contracts** and requires the employer's acceptance to take effect.

### Open-Ended Contract → Contract Termination by Notice (Article 75)

An employee on an open-ended contract does not "resign" in the legal sense — they **terminate the contract** with prior written notice. This termination does not require the employer's approval; it takes effect automatically once the notice period expires.

---

## Part Two: Terminating an Open-Ended Contract by Notice

### The Procedure

Either party may terminate an open-ended contract at any time for a legitimate reason, provided they give **prior written notice**:

| Terminating Party | Required Notice Period |
|-------------------|------------------------|
| Employee (monthly salary) | At least 30 days |
| Employer | At least 60 days |

> **Important:** No approval from the other party is required — notice plus the passage of time is sufficient. The employer may waive all or part of the notice period at their discretion.

### Leaving Without Notice

Leaving without serving the notice period triggers a financial consequence:
- Employee without notice: 30 days' pay may be deducted from their final settlement
- Employer without notice: must pay the employee an additional 60 days' compensation

### End-of-Service Benefit Upon Notice Termination

When an employee terminates an open-ended contract by notice, end-of-service benefits are calculated under Article 85:

| Years of Service | Benefit Entitlement |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Less than 2 years | No benefit |
| 2 to less than 5 years | One-third of full benefit |
| 5 to less than 10 years | Two-thirds of full benefit |
| 10 years or more | Full benefit |

## Part Three: Resignation from a Fixed-Term Contract — Article 79 bis

### Requirements for a Valid Resignation

For a resignation to be legally effective:
- **Written** — verbal resignation has no legal standing
- **Free from coercion** — a resignation under duress is void
- **Immediate** — the resignation cannot specify a future effective date
- **Submitted through official channels** at the organization

### Right to Withdraw

The employee may **withdraw their resignation within 7 days** of submission, provided two conditions are met simultaneously:
- The withdrawal occurs within the 7-day window
- The employer has not already accepted it

If either condition fails, the withdrawal is invalid.

### During the Resignation Period

Article 79 bis, paragraph 5, states explicitly: **"The employment contract remains in force during the resignation period, and both parties are obligated to fulfill all obligations arising from it."**

This means: the employee cannot be absent from work, and the employer cannot prevent the employee from attending.

## Part Four: How the Employer Responds to a Resignation

Article 79 bis defines four possible scenarios:

**Scenario 1 — Express Acceptance:**
The employer accepts in writing before 30 days have passed. The contract ends on the date of acceptance.

**Scenario 2 — Implied Acceptance:**
If **30 days pass** with no response, the resignation is considered **automatically accepted** by operation of law.

**Scenario 3 — Deferred Acceptance:**
The employer may **defer acceptance** for up to **60 additional days**, subject to three cumulative conditions:
1. A legitimate operational reason exists
2. A **written, reasoned notice** is prepared
3. This notice reaches the employee **before the initial 30-day period expires**

> **Note:** The deferral period is calculated from the date the written notice is delivered to the employee, not from the date of resignation.

**Scenario 4 — Rejection:**
The employer may reject the resignation. In this case, the employee is obligated to continue working until the contract's end date. If the employee leaves despite the rejection, they become liable for **compensation for the remaining contract period** under Article 77.

### End-of-Service Benefit Upon Resignation

Article 79 bis, paragraph 6, states: **"An employee whose contract ends by resignation is entitled to all rights provided under this Law."** This means the same Article 85 schedule applies:

| Years of Service | Benefit Entitlement |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Less than 2 years | No benefit |
| 2 to less than 5 years | One-third of full benefit |
| 5 to less than 10 years | Two-thirds of full benefit |
| 10 years or more | Full benefit |

---

## Part Five: What the Employer Cannot Do

- Ignore a resignation beyond 30 days — it becomes automatically accepted
- Delay final settlement beyond two weeks from the contract end date
- Issue an experience certificate containing information that damages the employee's reputation
- Refuse to issue an experience certificate or charge a fee for it

---

## Part Six: Your Complete Entitlements Upon Termination

Regardless of how the contract ends, the employee is entitled to:
- Remaining salary through the last actual working day
- End-of-service benefit per the schedule above
- Cash compensation for unused annual leave
- A free experience certificate containing no damaging information
- Full settlement within two weeks

---

## Part Seven: Immediate Departure Without Notice or Approval

Article 81 permits an employee to leave immediately in specific circumstances, most notably:
- The employer breaches contractual or legal obligations
- The employee is subjected to abuse or physical harm
- Continuing work poses a genuine risk to the employee's health or safety
- The employer misrepresented the role at the time of hiring

In these cases, the employee is entitled to the **full end-of-service benefit** regardless of years of service.

## Practical Takeaways from 14 Years in HR

The difference between "resignation" and "contract termination by notice" is not just terminology — it has direct legal consequences for both parties.

**For employees on fixed-term contracts:** Your resignation requires the employer's acceptance. Do not assume it takes effect automatically. Document everything in writing and keep proof of delivery.

**For employees on open-ended contracts:** You are terminating the contract by notice — no one's approval is needed. But serve the full notice period to avoid a deduction from your settlement.

**For employers:** Article 79 bis sets strict response deadlines. Silence equals acceptance after 30 days. Build a clear resignation policy into your internal work regulations — one that specifies how submissions are received and how dates are documented.

This amendment was not designed to favor one side. It was designed to replace ambiguity with clarity — protecting workers from exploited resignation letters, and protecting employers from sudden unexplained departures, in service of a more stable and fair labor market.
